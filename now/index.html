updated 2019-10-26

Listed below are categories that show what I’m spending most of my time doing. Ultimately, I am attempting to align these with my current vision for my career: To live in the intersection of data science and behavioral economics/decision science– to be a data-driven decision scientist.

Current projects (Work @ Consumers Energy)

  • Modeling customer experience during power outages as a function of the characteristics of the outage, weather, customers’ recent experience with outages, notifications sent to customer, and estimated restoration time. I am using various ML techniques to identify significant features, and regression models to quantify the impact.
  • Creating a generalized text mining analysis that can be used to analyze any free responses from customer survey data.
  • Tuning our speech analytics tool to better transcribe and categorize call recordings.

Current projects (Personal)

  • Working pro bono with our local children’s museum to provide analytical support to better organize and then get insights from their member data. Currently most of my time is facilitating a partnership between the museum and Michigan State University’s Master of Business Analytics students as their final semester project.
  • Learning how to do river fishing in Michigan which is much different from the fishing I did growing up in North Carolina. I had a goal to catch one salmon in 2019. I caught two. Now we are moving on to trying to target and catch steelhead and/or northern pike.

Things I’d like to work on

  • My family recently became licensed for foster-care. We are planning to participate in a transitional foster care program for unaccompanied minors arriving at the US-Mexico border. It was supposed to have already started and we’ve been preserving margin in our time and energy in anticipation of that.
